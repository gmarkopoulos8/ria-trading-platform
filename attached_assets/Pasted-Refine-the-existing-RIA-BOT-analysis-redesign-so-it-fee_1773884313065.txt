Refine the existing RIA BOT analysis redesign so it feels premium, bold, and highly legible, while still being visually appealing and modern.

Goal:
Make the analysis page feel like a polished institutional-grade market dashboard, but cleaner, more elegant, and more attractive than a pure terminal look.

Design direction:
Blend these qualities:
- hedge-fund / institutional dashboard clarity
- premium modern fintech polish
- strong visual hierarchy
- clean dark-mode aesthetic
- high readability
- subtle luxury feel
- visually appealing spacing and composition

Do NOT make it look old, cluttered, or overly technical.
Do NOT make it look like a raw developer dashboard.
Do NOT make it feel like a plain black-and-green terminal.
It should feel premium, sharp, modern, and credible.

Visual goals:
- the verdict should feel powerful and instantly scannable
- the score should feel important and prestigious
- the hold duration / stop loss / take profits should feel structured and easy to digest
- the overall layout should feel clean and high-end
- the page should feel trustworthy and professional

Style guidance:

1. Hero card styling
Redesign the Trade Verdict hero card to feel like the centerpiece of the page.

Requirements:
- large rounded card with strong spacing
- subtle depth using shadows, borders, and layered panels
- rich dark background with elegant contrast
- large BUY / SELL / WATCH label with premium badge treatment
- score displayed as a bold numeric focal point
- hold duration and risk/reward metrics styled as clean mini-panels or stat chips
- one-line thesis summary styled as a premium subtitle
- top reasons shown in compact elegant rows

2. Typography
Use strong typography hierarchy:
- very large headline-style verdict label
- bold score typography
- medium-weight labels for hold duration, stop loss, and take profit
- clean smaller secondary text for summary and supporting analysis
- excellent spacing between text elements

Typography should feel polished and intentional, not oversized for no reason.

3. Color usage
Keep the app dark-mode-first, but make it visually appealing.

Use:
- strong contrast for text
- elegant accent colors
- restrained but effective color coding for BUY / SELL / WATCH
- subtle surface variation between cards
- premium badge treatments

Color coding should feel refined, not cartoonish or overly bright.

4. Information layout
The page should feel balanced and breathable.

Layout priorities:
- the hero verdict card dominates the top of the page
- below it, use neat summary grids
- deeper analytics should sit in organized cards below
- maintain enough whitespace so the interface does not feel cramped
- support both large desktop displays and mobile/tablet responsiveness

5. UI components to refine
Polish these components visually:
- TradeVerdictHeroCard
- ScoreBadge
- HoldDurationBadge
- RiskRewardPanel
- AnalysisSummaryGrid
- VerdictReasonList
- Symbol header / title area
- Scanner result cards
- Paper-trade candidate cards

6. Verdict badge styling
Make BUY / SELL / WATCH look much more premium.

Requirements:
- large pill or badge styling
- bold but refined
- clear visual distinction
- should pop immediately when the page loads
- should feel like the central decision output

7. Score presentation
Make the score feel like a major feature, not a small stat.

Display suggestions:
- oversized numeric score
- optional ring, panel, or badge treatment
- concise interpretation underneath
- visually paired with confidence and risk

8. Risk/reward presentation
Make Suggested Hold, Stop Loss, Take Profit 1, and Take Profit 2 look like executive summary metrics.

Requirements:
- each should be visually separated
- easy to read at a glance
- consistent spacing and typography
- feel like structured decision metrics rather than normal body text

9. Premium motion and interaction
Add tasteful polish where appropriate:
- smooth hover states
- soft transitions
- refined loading skeletons
- subtle animated emphasis on verdict/score cards
- polished empty states and no-data states

Avoid excessive animation or anything flashy.

10. Charts and supporting data
The chart and deeper technical data should remain visually subordinate to the verdict, but still polished.

Requirements:
- clean panel framing
- subtle borders
- good spacing
- easy-to-read labels
- visually connected to the hero analysis card

11. Overall brand feel
The final result should feel like:
- a premium trading intelligence product
- visually attractive enough for investor demos
- clear enough for daily use
- modern enough to compete with high-end fintech products

It should feel closer to:
- premium fintech SaaS
- institutional research terminal with modern design
- elegant dark dashboard

It should NOT feel like:
- a retro terminal
- a crypto gambling app
- a cluttered brokerage screen
- a generic admin template

12. Deliverable
Refine the existing UI so the verdict, score, hold duration, stop loss, and take profits are visually striking, premium, and easy to understand immediately, while the full page remains beautiful, modern, and highly usable.
